Broken System. Outdated Approach.

For over fifty years, Construction Management (CM) and other delivery methods have failed to deliver value. Instead, they've created a system that rewards inefficiency rather than value. The core issue? Cost-plus and billable-hour contracts that incentivize waste and inflate budgets.

"The contractor has no real incentive to complete the job quickly or cheaply – the longer he takes and more he spends, the larger his profit — not necessarily a good deal for the owner." — Building Advisor

This isn't just about rising costs—it's about a fundamental misalignment of interests. When contractors and consultants make more money from longer timelines and higher costs, the result is predictable: projects that cost more and take longer than necessary.

Proven System. Ready Now.

In the 1980s, Toyota forced a shift in American manufacturing by proving that even simple products like cars benefit from a systems-based approach to improving value and reducing waste. If cars require a system, how much more do buildings—the world's largest, most complex products? Now, after decades of research and development, Genesis and its technology partner Building CATALYST bring that same proven management philosophy to construction planning, design, and delivery. All it takes is a few bold innovators—owners, consultants, designers, and builders—willing to lead the change.

1940s – World War II

Deming Introduces Statistical Quality Control

As U.S. industries scaled up for war, W. Edwards Deming introduced statistical process control (SPC) to reduce waste and improve quality. Mass production without mass defect.

1947

Deming Invited to Post-War Japan

General MacArthur's team invited Deming to help rebuild Japan's economy, introducing process-focused, long-term thinking. Fix the process, not the people.

1950-1960

Japan Acts on Deming's Vision

highlighting how Japan institutionalized Deming's teachings and developed practices like Kaizen and Just-in-Time manufacturing.

1980

NBC Documentary: "If Japan Can... Why Can't We?"

This program shocked Americans, revealing Japan's manufacturing superiority and crediting Deming, triggering U.S. interest. Deming re-enters U.S. spotlight.

1986

Toyota Sets the Standard

Toyota's system, built on Deming's principles, becomes a global model of quality. Continuous improvement revolutionizes manufacturing.

1999

Genesis is Founded

Created as a pilot for Steelcase and Workstage, Genesis brings Deming's system to construction. Applying proven methods to a new sector.
